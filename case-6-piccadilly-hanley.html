Case Study · Title & Lease Resolution

6 Piccadilly, Hanley.

Post-acquisition advice to a private investor facing title irregularities, communal-area disputes, and subsequent lease termination. Multi-stage representation from operational fix-up through to lease exit and re-letting strategy.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Retail investment acquired at auction
  • Title and ownership discrepancies identified post-acquisition
  • Communal management dispute involving multiple owners
  • Lease termination negotiations with occupational tenant
  • Dilapidations and re-letting advice provided
  • Multi-stage instruction across operational and lease issues
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The instruction.

I was instructed by a private investor following their purchase of 6 Piccadilly, Hanley at auction.

Following completion, it became apparent that the Land Registry information relied upon during the acquisition was outdated. The client had believed they were acquiring one part of a larger property block under single ownership. However, further investigation revealed that the wider building had been split into multiple titles and sold separately at recent auctions.

This had created confusion regarding responsibility for communal areas within the building, particularly a shared bin store and waste management arrangements.

02 · Our role
What I did.

What the work involved.

A detailed investigation into the ownership structure of the wider building, liaising with neighbouring owners and reviewing historic arrangements relating to the communal areas.

During this process it became clear that a management company had originally been intended to oversee the communal parts, however this had not been implemented following the various property sales. The lack of clarity had resulted in the bin store becoming unmanaged, leading to a build-up of waste and ongoing dispute between the various property owners.

I worked with the client, neighbouring owners and the occupational tenant to resolve the immediate operational issues, ensuring excess waste was removed and establishing clarity regarding future management responsibilities.

At a later stage, the client instructed me again following receipt of formal notice from the tenant regarding their intention not to renew the lease. I advised the landlord on their options under the lease and the Landlord & Tenant Act, including the possibility of negotiating revised lease terms to retain the tenant on improved conditions.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

The outcome.

I entered into negotiations with the tenant to explore a lease renewal. However, the tenant ultimately confirmed their decision to cease trading from the premises.

I therefore advised the client on the appropriate steps to manage the lease expiry, including coordination of final rent and service charge matters, and advice relating to the tenant's dilapidations obligations.

Following the tenant's departure, I provided strategic advice on the client's re-letting options and future management of the property, ensuring the landlord was fully protected and positioned to move forward with the asset.
