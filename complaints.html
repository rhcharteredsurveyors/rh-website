A formal process

Complaints Handling Procedure.

If something isn't working, I want to know about it, and I want to address it properly.

Section 01
My commitment

The starting point is direct contact.

RH Chartered Surveyors is regulated by the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS). Every regulated firm is required to operate a written complaints handling procedure. This is mine.

The practice is run by me personally. If you're unhappy with any aspect of my work, the fastest way to resolve it is usually a conversation. I'd rather hear about something early, while it can still be put right, than after the fact. Most concerns are resolved in that first conversation.

But sometimes a formal process is the right route. That's what this procedure is for: a clear, time-bound, two-stage path for when something needs to be looked at properly. There's no charge for raising a complaint with me, and it doesn't affect any other rights you may have.

Section 02
Stage 1: Internal complaint

How to raise a formal complaint.

Put your complaint in writing and send it to me. Please include enough detail for me to investigate properly: what happened, when, who was involved, and what outcome you're looking for.

Send your complaint to:
Rob Haigh MRICS, Principal
RH Chartered Surveyors
12A Southwell Business Centre, Crew Lane, Southwell, Nottinghamshire NG25 0TX
Or by email: rob@rhcharteredsurveyors.co.uk

What happens then:

  1. Acknowledgement within 3 working days. I'll confirm in writing that I've received your complaint, summarise my understanding of it, and tell you who is handling it.
  2. Investigation. I'll review the file, talk to anyone relevant, and consider the points you've raised. If I need more information from you, I'll come back to you.
  3. Full written response within 28 days of acknowledgement. This will set out my findings, whether the complaint is upheld in whole or in part, and what I propose to do about it. If the investigation is genuinely taking longer for good reason, I'll write to explain why and give a revised date.
Section 03
Stage 2: Independent dispute resolution

If we can't resolve it together.

If you're not satisfied with my final written response, or if more than eight weeks have passed since you first raised the complaint with me and the matter remains unresolved, you can refer the matter to independent dispute resolution.

For matters that can't be resolved between us, I have nominated the RICS Dispute Resolution Service (DRS) as my independent dispute resolution provider. DRS is operated by RICS, is independent of this firm, and is the long established provider of alternative dispute resolution in land, property and construction.

To take a matter to DRS, contact them directly using the details below. They operate their own process, scheme rules and fees, which you can review on their website. They will expect to see that you've completed Stage 1 of this procedure, or that more than eight weeks have passed without resolution.

Independent dispute resolution provider:
RICS Dispute Resolution Service (DRS)
Telephone: 020 7334 3806
Email: drs@rics.org
Web: www.rics.org/dispute-resolution-service

DRS offers a range of options including mediation, arbitration, adjudication and expert determination. Depending on the route used, the outcome may be binding on both parties. I will cooperate fully with the process and abide by any binding decision.

Section 04
Records and review

How complaints are tracked.

A written record of every formal complaint is kept on the firm's complaints register, including the nature of the complaint, the response, and any action taken. The register is reviewed regularly to identify patterns and improve the service.

Records are retained in line with RICS requirements and in compliance with the firm's privacy policy. See the Privacy Policy for how personal data is handled.

Section 05
Other routes

What this procedure doesn't replace.

This procedure is for complaints about the service provided by RH Chartered Surveyors. It does not replace any other rights you may have, including your right to take legal action or to refer professional conduct concerns directly to RICS.

To raise concerns about professional conduct:
RICS Regulation handles concerns about the professional conduct of its members and regulated firms. You can report a concern directly to RICS at www.rics.org/regulation/reporting-concerns.

Version and updates
