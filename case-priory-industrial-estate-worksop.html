Case Study · Lease Renewal Strategy

Priory Industrial Estate, Worksop.

Tenant market rent review across multi-let industrial. Honest advice that the proposed rent was actually fair, refocusing energy onto the wider lease terms where real value lay.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Total passing rent agreed at £26,000 per annum
  • 4,871 sq ft multi-let industrial and ancillary space
  • Consistent tone at £5.25 per sq ft across garages
  • No defensible position to reduce rent further
  • Strategic advice focused on lease terms
  • Rent-free period, schedule of condition and break secured
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The instruction.

I was instructed by the tenant to review the proposed rent on a lease renewal at Priory Industrial Estate, Worksop.

The landlord's agent had put forward a revised rent of £26,000 per annum, supported by comparable evidence. The client wanted to understand whether there was scope to challenge this and reduce their overall liability.

02 · Our role
What I did.

What the work involved.

A detailed review of the landlord's evidence, alongside my own comparable data from similar instructions.

The assessment provided by the landlord's agent was well structured and, importantly, sensibly adjusted to reflect differences between the subject units and the comparables. The adopted rate of £5.25 per sq ft across the garage units was consistent and, while there were minor arguments around size differentials, these had little meaningful impact on the overall rental figure.

I scrutinised the inclusion of the flat within the same rate-per-sq-ft analysis. In isolation this is not how I would typically assess residential accommodation. However, when broken back to a monthly figure, the rent attributed to the flat appeared low, which in effect balanced the overall assessment.

On that basis, my advice was clear. There was no strong or defensible position to negotiate the rent down further. Rather than pursuing a weak argument on rent, I advised shifting focus to the wider lease terms.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

The outcome.

The client proceeded with the renewal at £26,000 per annum, confident that the rent reflected the current market position.

More importantly, my advice ensured the negotiation focused on areas that genuinely mattered, including securing a rent-free period, limiting repairing liability through a schedule of condition, protecting against future EPC-related costs, and maintaining flexibility through a break option.

A well-balanced lease renewal where the client avoided wasting time and cost on an unwinnable rent argument, and instead secured stronger overall terms aligned with long-term occupation.
