Case Study · Office Re-letting

Pride Park, Re-letting Strategy.

Office accommodation to re-let in a competitive Derby market. Positioning, presentation and joint agency executed in coordination, bringing the asset to market without delay following tenant departure.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Units 4–6 Stadium Business Court, Pride Park, Derby
  • Office accommodation, 4,172 sq ft
  • Quoting rent £62,580 per annum
  • Joint agency instruction with FHP Derby
  • Marketing coordinated with refurbishment programme
  • Brought to market without vacancy gap
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The marketing brief.

Units 4–6 Stadium Business Court, Pride Park, Derby had to be brought to market quickly and properly. A tenant had exercised their break option, and two private landlords needed the accommodation re-let in a competitive Derby office market.

The accommodation needed work: refurbishment to maximise lettability, presentation to attract the right occupier, and a quoting strategy that reflected the market without underselling the asset.

The brief wasn't just "put it on the market." It was position the asset, prepare it, and run the marketing alongside refurbishment so the vacancy gap was minimised.

02 · Our role
What I did.

How the marketing was run.

Advice on quoting strategy, market positioning and refurbishment scope came first. Quoting too high meant the property sat on the market and lost momentum. Quoting too low meant under-extracting value from the lease event. £62,580 per annum reflected the lettable value of the accommodation post-refurbishment, supported by comparable evidence across Pride Park.

On reach, I recommended joint agency with FHP Derby. Pride Park is FHP's home market. Their occupier database for Derby offices is deeper than any single firm could match from outside, and joint marketing materially increases the chance of finding the right tenant quickly.

I remained the landlords' primary point of contact throughout, handling the relationship, coordinating with the refurbishment contractors, and managing the marketing strategy alongside FHP. The landlords didn't have to choose between coverage and a single advisor. They got both.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

How the asset is performing.

The property was brought to market immediately following the tenant's departure, with refurbishment and marketing running in parallel rather than sequentially.

Marketing is active. The instruction is ongoing. The asset is being worked, not waiting.
