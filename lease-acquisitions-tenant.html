What this work involves
Honest reading of the situation.

Lease events sit on a spectrum.
Some are clean. Others carry hidden cost, or hidden opportunity.

Some lease events are straightforward: you've found premises and need someone to negotiate the lease properly, or your break clause aligns with your plans and the job is exiting cleanly. Those are good days.

Others carry risk you might not have spotted: a deal with dilapidations exposure you haven't accounted for, a break clause whose conditions could void the notice if missed, a surrender priced well above what a properly negotiated exit would actually cost.

My job is to read the situation honestly, tell you plainly where the risks and opportunities sit, and handle the negotiation so you're not navigating it alone.

Who this is for
Tenants at a lease event.

Businesses taking on new premises and wanting someone to negotiate the lease properly before they sign. Tenants with a break clause coming up who want to make sure they exercise it correctly. Anyone facing an early exit, or being approached for one, and wanting to know what it should actually cost.

If you're at a lease event and you're not sure what you're entitled to, get in touch.

Common situations I'm instructed on.

Acquisitions and exits look very different depending on the circumstances. These are the six scenarios I'm most often instructed on.

01
Taking on a new lease.
The lease you sign today shapes your cost base and your flexibility for years. I'll review the proposed terms before you commit, negotiate rent and incentives properly, push for the flexibility your business will need in three years' time, and see it through to signed lease without loose ends.
02
Exercising a break clause.
Breaks fail on technicalities more often than on strategy: notice period, rent paid to date, vacant possession conditions. One missed condition and you're tied in for the remainder of the term. I'll work through the lease properly, get the notice and the compliance right, and exit you cleanly.
03
Regearing a lease mid-term.
Sometimes the right answer isn't exiting. It's reshaping the deal in place. A regear typically trades a rent reduction or restructure against something the landlord values: a longer term, the removal of a break clause, redevelopment rights. Done properly, both sides come away with something they wanted.
04
Negotiating an early surrender.
Sometimes the right outcome is leaving early and paying a sensible figure to do so. Surrender negotiations often open at numbers well above what a properly represented tenant would pay. Getting the figure right can save tens of thousands.
05
Where the landlord wants possession back.
If a landlord is approaching you to surrender early (typically for redevelopment or their own use), the situation is different. They usually cover legal and surveyor fees, and you may be entitled to compensation. Tenants regularly accept these approaches without realising what they're entitled to. Get advice before you agree to anything.
06
Assignment or sub-letting.
When you need to pass the lease on rather than break it, I'll handle the negotiation with the landlord, the documentation, and the relationship with the incoming party.
