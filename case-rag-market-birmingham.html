Case Study · Compensation Strategy

Unit 9, Rag Market, Birmingham.

Repositioned a redevelopment-driven early termination from a landlord-led process into a tenant-led negotiation. Compensation package secured, with landlord covering all professional fees.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Retail unit at Rag Market, Birmingham
  • Lease due to expire within 12 months
  • Landlord seeking early possession for redevelopment
  • Tenant-led negotiations opened
  • Landlord covering legal and professional fees
  • Compensation strategy ongoing
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The instruction.

I was instructed by a private tenant client following approach from the landlord's agent seeking an early termination of the lease to facilitate redevelopment of the wider site.

The tenant had been in long-term occupation, but the lease was due to expire within the next 12 months.

02 · Our role
What I did.

What the work involved.

I advised the client on the strategic position, highlighting that if they did not engage with the landlord's proposal, they would likely be refused a new lease at expiry. This would result in the tenant vacating with no compensation, despite the landlord's requirement for early possession.

My advice was that this situation presented an opportunity to negotiate a compensation package for early surrender, ensure the tenant's position was fully protected, and secure professional representation at no cost, as landlords in these scenarios typically cover legal and surveyor fees.

Following instruction, I opened negotiations with the landlord's agent to establish timescales and the basis of compensation.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

The outcome.

Negotiations are ongoing, with the work focused on maximising the compensation available to the tenant in return for early possession.

The client is now in a controlled and informed position, ensuring that any exit from the property is aligned with their best interests, rather than dictated by the landlord's programme.
