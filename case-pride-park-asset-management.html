Case Study · Multi-Discipline Coordination

Pride Park, Asset Repositioning.

One asset, four disciplines, one relationship. Strategic review, legal coordination, dilapidations and re-letting handled by a single advisor over the life of a complete repositioning.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Three office units at Pride Park, Derby
  • Total floor area 4,172 sq ft
  • Lease event anticipated and prepared for in advance
  • Multi-discipline coordination across one client relationship
  • Legal, dilapidations, refurbishment, and letting handled in sequence
  • No referrals out: single point of contact throughout
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The shape of the instruction.

When the two landlords of Units 4–6 Stadium Business Court, Pride Park, Derby first instructed me, the brief was a strategic review. By the time the asset is fully repositioned, the same instruction will have spanned five distinct disciplines, none of which required them to onboard a new advisor along the way.

This is the work strategic asset management exists to do. Not one transaction. The whole asset, over time, handled by one person who knows it.

02 · Our role
What I did.

How the disciplines connected.

It started with a strategic review. The landlords had recently agreed a deed of variation introducing a rolling tenant break, and wanted an independent view of their position. The review flagged the risk that the break would be exercised, and laid out what preparation should look like if it was.

When the tenant did exercise the break (exactly as anticipated), the same instruction shifted into execution mode. I oversaw the legal representation, brought in a specialist dilapidations consultant from my network, and coordinated the refurbishment programme across multiple contractors.

In parallel, the re-letting strategy was developed: quoting, positioning, marketing reach, and a joint agency arrangement with FHP Derby to maximise occupier coverage in the Derby market.

Each phase informed the next. The dilapidations approach was shaped by what the refurbishment needed to deliver. The refurbishment was scoped against the rental position the market would pay. The marketing strategy was timed against when the accommodation would actually be lettable. None of this works as well when each phase is handed off to a different firm.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

Where it goes from here.

The asset is now in the active repositioning phase. Refurbishment underway, marketing live, the legal and dilapidations work completed.

The landlords have had one relationship throughout, across what would normally be three or four separate firms. The decisions are informed by everything that came before them. The asset is being shepherded, not project-managed in pieces.

This is what one-advisor strategic asset management produces: continuity, context, and a property strategy that compounds over time.
