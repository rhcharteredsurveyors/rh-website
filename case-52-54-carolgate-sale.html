Case Study · Investment Disposal

52–54 Carolgate, Retford.

Sale of a £22,000 pa town centre retail investment to a local investor at 10% gross initial yield. Exchange and completion achieved within seven weeks of solicitors being instructed.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Passing rent £22,000 per annum
  • Freehold retail investment sale
  • Sale price £215,000
  • Net initial yield approximately 10%
  • Joint agency instruction
  • Exchange and completion in seven weeks
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The instruction.

I acted as joint agent in the sale of 52–54 Carolgate, Retford, a town centre retail investment situated on the town's primary pedestrianised shopping street.

The property comprises a two-storey retail building let to Roman Originals PLC, producing a passing rent of £22,000 per annum, offering investors a well-located income producing asset within the heart of the town centre.

02 · Our role
What I did.

What the work involved.

A central role in progressing the transaction, acting as the primary point of contact during negotiations.

I led discussions between the vendor, purchaser and respective professional teams, negotiating the agreed terms of the sale and overseeing the transaction through the legal process to completion.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

The outcome.

The property was successfully sold to a local investor purchaser for £215,000, reflecting a net initial yield of approximately 10%.

Once terms were agreed, the transaction progressed efficiently, with exchange and completion achieved within seven weeks of solicitors being instructed.

Following completion, the purchaser subsequently instructed me to advise on the acquisition of another investment property on Carolgate, demonstrating the strength of the relationship established through the transaction.
