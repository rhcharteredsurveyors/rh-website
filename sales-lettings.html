What this work involves
More than a sign on the wall.

Selling or letting commercial property is about more than putting a sign up.

Pricing, timing, tenant selection, lease structure. Each materially affects what you end up with. A rushed letting to the wrong tenant can cost more than a considered void. A sale priced on hope rather than evidence sits on the market and quietly loses value.

My approach is advisor-first, transaction-second.

The work starts with strategy. How the asset should be positioned, who it's marketed to, what it should achieve, when it should go to market. The marketing activity follows from that, not the other way around.

Who this is for
Landlords with an asset to sell or let.

Landlords with an asset to sell or let, whether that's a single property or part of a wider portfolio disposal. Property owners who want strategy and commercial judgement ahead of a For Sale board. Anyone who wants to understand what their asset should achieve before it goes to market.

If that sounds like you, get in touch.

How I approach it.

Six principles that shape every sales or lettings instruction, whether a single property or a portfolio disposal.

01
Strategy first.
How the asset should be positioned, who's the likely buyer or tenant, what it's genuinely worth, when it should go to market. The strategic decisions made before launch shape what's possible afterwards.
02
Position it correctly.
Pricing, presentation, market tone. Removing friction or resolving issues before they become negotiating leverage for the other side.
03
The right approach for the right asset.
Some assets need broad market coverage. Others are best sold or let quietly to a known local party or special purchaser. I advise on which, and execute accordingly.
04
Reach beyond a single firm.
Some instructions benefit from broader market coverage than any one practice can deliver. When that's the right call, I'll bring in a joint agent. The client relationship stays with me throughout.
05
Qualify carefully.
Whether it's a buyer or a tenant, covenant, intentions, and financial position all matter. The wrong party on the line stores up problems.
06
Lead the negotiation through to completion.
Direct contact with the other side, solicitor liaison, commercial terms properly reflected in the legal documentation. You're kept informed at the level of detail you want.
An honest position

Hope isn't a strategy.

A sale priced on hope rather than evidence sits on the market and quietly loses value. A letting rushed to fill a void can lock in a tenant whose performance you can't guarantee.

Strategy upfront doesn't remove all uncertainty. But it lets you make decisions with eyes open, rather than fingers crossed.
