Case Study · Lease Restructure

103 Ilkeston Road, Nottingham.

Restructured the lease on an underperforming mixed-use asset to support long-term redevelopment plans. Rent uplifted from £8,000 to £12,000, with a rolling mutual break preserving future flexibility.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Mixed-use investment property in Nottingham
  • Ground floor retail accommodation with residential upper floors
  • Existing lease holding over from 2015
  • Rent increased from £8,000 to £12,000 per annum
  • Rolling mutual break introduced after six months
  • Flexible lease structure pending future redevelopment
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The instruction.

I was instructed by an investor landlord in relation to a mixed-use property at 103 Ilkeston Road, Nottingham.

The client intended to pursue future redevelopment of the upper floors to provide student accommodation, however the property remained occupied under an expired lease at a historically low passing rent.

The client required advice on how the occupational arrangements could be restructured to improve the short-term income position whilst preserving flexibility for future redevelopment.

02 · Our role
What I did.

What the work involved.

A strategic review of the existing lease structure, rental position and wider asset strategy for the property.

I advised on how the occupational arrangements could be revised to support the client's longer-term redevelopment objectives whilst maintaining interim income and reducing occupational risk.

The role included negotiation of revised lease terms directly with the tenant, introduction and oversight of legal representation, and coordination of the lease documentation process through to completion. Throughout negotiations, I balanced redevelopment flexibility against the practical requirements of the existing occupier.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

The outcome.

A revised lease structure was implemented securing an immediate rental increase from £8,000 per annum to £12,000 per annum.

The agreed lease incorporated a rolling mutual break option after six months, allowing the landlord to maintain flexibility whilst progressing future redevelopment and planning proposals.

The instruction enabled the client to improve short-term income performance whilst preserving strategic control of the asset ahead of future repositioning works.
