Case Study · Lease Dispute

19–21 Baldertongate, Newark.

Resolved a complex tenant dispute involving holding-over occupation, unauthorised alterations and defective repair works. 10-year lease secured with 20% rental uplift instead of pursuing strict enforcement.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Retail unit in Newark town centre
  • Tenant holding over on expired lease
  • Previous rent £12,000 per annum
  • New lease agreed from £12,500 rising to £14,500
  • 10-year lease term secured
  • Roof repairs completed at approx. £4,000
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The instruction.

I was instructed by a private landlord client in relation to a complex lease situation at 19–21 Baldertongate, Newark.

The tenant was holding over on an expired lease, had undertaken significant alterations without consent, and had previously arranged roof repairs through a contractor whose work had proven defective.

The landlord was facing a difficult position, balancing repair liability, lease renewal negotiations and the risk of vacancy.

02 · Our role
What I did.

What the work involved.

A detailed review of both the technical and leasehold position, advising the client on their options.

I introduced a specialist roofing contractor from my network to inspect the property and provide an independent assessment. This confirmed that the main roof structure was sound, defects were isolated to poor detailing and perimeter termination, and a targeted repair solution could be implemented without full replacement.

I advised the client that, while the lease placed repairing liability on the tenant, enforcing this strictly carried risk. The tenant was not a strong covenant, and a hard-line approach could have resulted in vacancy, legal costs and an unresolved building issue.

My advice was to take a pragmatic, commercial approach, using the roof works as part of a wider negotiation strategy to secure a new long-term lease, improve the passing rent, and ensure ongoing occupation.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

The outcome.

The roof was repaired and brought into a watertight condition, removing an immediate risk to the property.

A new 10-year lease was agreed, with rent increasing from £12,500 per annum to £14,500, representing a near 20% uplift on the previous passing rent.

The agreed deal secured long-term income, continued occupation of a specialist tenant, and avoidance of vacancy in a challenging retail location. This was particularly important given the level of tenant fit-out, which would have made re-letting difficult and increased the risk of dilapidations issues. A potentially problematic situation turned into a stable, income-producing investment.
