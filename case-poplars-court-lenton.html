Case Study · Rates & Strategy

11 & 13 Poplars Court, Lenton.

Identified a technical route to reduce business rates from £14,700 to £1,800 per annum, but flagged the risk of triggering reassessment. Honest downside-first advice led the client to relocate instead.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Two adjoining office units (freehold ownership)
  • Combined rates liability c. £14,700 per annum
  • Potential reduction to c. £1,800 per annum identified
  • Multiple risk scenarios assessed
  • Strategic advice led to relocation decision
  • High-risk mitigation strategy avoided
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The instruction.

I was instructed to review the business rates position across two adjoining office units, with the client keen to understand whether there was an opportunity to reduce their ongoing costs.

While the focus was initially on business rates, the instruction quickly evolved into a broader strategic review of their occupation and future plans.

02 · Our role
What I did.

What the work involved.

A detailed analysis of the existing assessments, measurement data and valuation approach adopted by the VOA, alongside comparable evidence within the local market.

The findings were fairly balanced. On the face of it, the rateable values appeared broadly in line with market evidence, meaning a straightforward challenge on valuation tone was unlikely to deliver a meaningful reduction.

However, I did identify a more technical route which could have significantly reduced the liability, potentially bringing it down from around £14,700 per annum to circa £1,800.

The key point was that this was not a risk-free opportunity. Given the physical relationship between the two units, there was a real possibility that any challenge could trigger a reassessment, leading to the properties being merged into a single hereditament. This would have removed eligibility for SBRR entirely and could have resulted in a higher overall liability.

Rather than simply presenting the potential saving, I set out the full range of outcomes, including the realistic downside scenarios.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

The outcome.

Armed with a clear understanding of both the opportunity and the risk, the client chose not to pursue a formal challenge.

Instead, they used my advice to take a step back and reassess their wider occupational strategy, ultimately deciding to acquire alternative premises and relocate.

This avoided the uncertainty of a potentially adverse appeal process and ensured their property costs were aligned with their long-term business objectives.
