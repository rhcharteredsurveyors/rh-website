Case Study · Forward Rates Planning

Multi-Property Industrial Portfolio.

Forward-looking review of business rates across two interconnected industrial buildings. Identified £20,000+ per annum future exposure and advised against high-risk mitigation strategies.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Two interconnected industrial buildings
  • Three separate leasehold interests
  • One building not currently assessed for rates
  • Potential future liability in excess of £20,000 per annum
  • Mitigation strategy identified but not pursued
  • Forward planning enabled for reassessment
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The instruction.

I was instructed to review the business rates position across two interconnected industrial properties occupied by a single business.

One building was fully assessed but held under multiple leases, while the second, despite being refurbished and occupied, was not currently listed for business rates.

The client wanted clarity on their current position and a forward-looking view of their likely exposure.

02 · Our role
What I did.

What the work involved.

A detailed analysis of the existing rating assessment, lease structure, and physical configuration of the buildings, alongside comparable evidence within the relevant valuation scheme.

The review identified that the key issue was not the current liability, but what was likely to happen next. The unassessed building was highly likely to be brought back into rating following occupation, creating a new and material cost. At the same time, the assessed building appeared to be under-valued relative to its condition and use, meaning any formal review by the VOA could result in a significant increase.

I also explored the potential to restructure the existing assessment to benefit from Small Business Rates Relief. While this presented a theoretical opportunity to reduce liability, it would likely trigger a reassessment, introducing a real risk of increasing the overall liability instead.

My advice was clear: while a saving was technically possible, the risk of worsening the client's position outweighed the potential benefit.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

The outcome.

The client was provided with a clear, commercially grounded understanding of both their current and future business rates exposure.

Rather than pursuing a high-risk mitigation strategy, they made an informed decision to maintain the existing position, avoiding the potential for a significant increase in liability.

Crucially, they were able to plan ahead for the reassessment of the second property and incorporate this into their wider business strategy, ensuring there were no unexpected cost shocks.
