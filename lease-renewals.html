What this work involves
More than the number.

On paper, a lease event is a negotiation about a number.
In practice, it rarely is.

A rent review you settle today becomes the evidence used against you at the next review. A lease renewal isn't just a renewed term. It's a set of rights, repairs, and break options that'll shape your next disposal.

My job is to see the whole picture, not just the number on the table.

Sometimes that means handling a clean, contained instruction quickly and well. Sometimes it means recognising that what looks like a lease event is actually the start of a wider decision about the asset.

Who this is for
Landlords with a lease event ahead.

Landlords with an upcoming rent review or lease renewal, whether that's a straightforward negotiation or a more complex situation involving tenant strategy, covenant considerations, or wider portfolio objectives.

If a lease event is on the horizon and you want to approach it with a clear strategy rather than react when it arrives, get in touch.

How I approach it.

Six principles that shape every lease renewal and rent review I take on, for any asset, in any market, of any complexity.

01
Read the lease properly.
Every variation, side letter, and historical amendment. The critical leverage is often buried in a clause nobody's looked at for years.
02
Give you a plain commercial view.
Some reviews and renewals are worth pushing hard. Some aren't. I'll tell you honestly which is which.
03
Think three moves ahead.
Today's rent review is evidence for the next one. Today's renewal shapes your next disposal. I'll flag consequences before we commit.
04
Lead negotiations directly.
With the tenant, their agent, or their legal team. You're kept informed at the level of detail that suits you.
05
See it through.
Heads of terms, solicitor liaison, signed documentation. The work isn't finished until the paperwork reflects the deal.
06
Act before the clock runs down.
Lease events reward preparation. Coming to me early means we shape the negotiation rather than react to someone else's opening position.
