What this work involves
The whole portfolio, one advisor.

Most landlords with a portfolio don't need a surveyor for one job.

They need someone who knows the whole picture. Every asset, every lease, every tenant, every upcoming decision. And who's thinking about it between phone calls, not just on them.

That's what Strategic Asset Management is.

I act as your lead advisor across everything: lease events, acquisitions, disposals, management, valuations, rates, disputes. The lot. No referrals to another department. No meeting a new face for each matter.

One relationship, across every asset you own, for as long as you need me.

Who this is for
Sophisticated property holders.

Property companies. High-net-worth individuals with several assets. Businesses whose property is a material part of their wealth.

If your portfolio is too valuable, too active, or too complex to manage instruction-by-instruction, this is the relationship that works.

How I approach it.

Six principles that define the difference between transactional surveying and a strategic asset management relationship.

01
Know your assets as well as you do.
I'll work through each property, lease and tenant until I have the same mental picture you have. The relationship starts here.
02
Stay ahead of the lease calendar.
I track upcoming events and flag the ones that need decisions taken in good time. Lease events handled early are always better than lease events handled late.
03
Notice what you're not seeing.
Underperforming assets, rates exposure, tenants heading for trouble, acquisitions that fit your thesis, disposals worth thinking about. Part of the job is staying alert on your behalf.
04
Be the first call.
When something happens, you ring me. I tell you what I think, plainly. We decide what to do. No filter, no team meeting required.
05
Handle the execution.
When an instruction lands, you don't start from scratch with someone else. I know the asset, the portfolio, and how you like to work.
06
Built for the long term.
This isn't a one-off instruction. The value of the relationship builds over time, and the longer we work together, the more useful I become.
An honest position
