Privacy Policy.

How RH Chartered Surveyors collects, uses, and protects your personal information.
Plain English. No surprises.

01 · Overview
Who this applies to.

About this policy.

This Privacy Policy explains how RH Chartered Surveyors (a trading name of Haigh & Haigh Properties Limited, company number 12302429) collects, uses, stores, and protects personal information.

It applies to anyone who interacts with the practice, including clients, prospective clients, suppliers, and visitors to this website. We process personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

03 · Data collected
What we hold.

What information we collect.

The practice collects only the personal information necessary to deliver professional services and meet regulatory obligations. This typically includes:

  • Contact informationname, postal address, email address, and telephone number provided by clients, prospects, or suppliers.
  • Professional informationdetails of properties, leases, tenancies, valuations, and other matters relevant to the work being undertaken.
  • Financial informationlimited to information necessary for the conduct of professional services, such as rateable values, rents, or transaction details.
  • Identity verificationdocuments and information collected to satisfy Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Know Your Client (KYC) requirements.
  • Correspondenceemails, messages, and other written communications between the practice and clients or third parties.

The website uses Plausible Analytics, a privacy-friendly analytics service, to understand how visitors find and use the site. Plausible does not use cookies. It does not collect, retain, or share any personal data, and it does not track visitors across other websites. The data collected is aggregated and anonymous: page views, referring website, country (derived from IP address, which is not retained), device type, and browser. This information is used solely to understand which content is useful, where visitors come from, and how to improve the site.

Plausible Analytics is GDPR-compliant by design and does not require consent banners. More information about Plausible's data practices is available at plausible.io/data-policy.

04 · Purpose
Why we use it.

How information is used.

Personal information is used only for the purposes for which it was collected. These purposes include:

  • Providing the professional services agreed with the client
  • Communicating with clients, prospects, and other parties involved in a matter
  • Issuing invoices and managing accounting records
  • Complying with regulatory obligations, including those of RICS and HMRC
  • Conducting AML and conflict-of-interest checks before accepting instructions
  • Maintaining records required by professional standards or law
05 · Lawful basis
Legal grounds for processing.

Why this is lawful.

Under the UK GDPR, the practice must have a lawful basis for processing personal data. We rely on the following:

  • Performance of a contractfor processing data necessary to deliver agreed professional services to clients.
  • Legal obligationfor processing required by law, including AML/KYC checks under the Money Laundering Regulations 2017 and tax record-keeping under HMRC requirements.
  • Legitimate interestsfor operational matters such as responding to enquiries, maintaining records of past instructions, and protecting the practice in the event of a dispute. The practice's interests are balanced against the rights and freedoms of the data subject.
  • Consentwhere applicable, for any optional communications such as newsletters or marketing (none currently active).
06 · Sharing
Who else sees the data.

Sharing with third parties.

Personal data is not sold, rented, or shared for marketing purposes. It may be shared with the following categories of recipients, only where necessary and with appropriate safeguards in place:

  • Professional advisorssolicitors, accountants, planning consultants, or other surveyors instructed on the same matter, where coordination is required.
  • Regulatory and tax bodiesHMRC, RICS, and other authorities where required by law.
  • AML verification providersthird-party services used to conduct identity and beneficial ownership checks, where applicable.
  • Joint agentswhere the practice acts jointly with another firm on a marketing instruction or transaction.
  • IT and service providersincluding email, cloud storage, and document management providers, all bound by appropriate data processing agreements.

The practice does not transfer personal data outside the UK or European Economic Area unless adequate protections are in place.

07 · Retention
How long data is kept.

Data retention.

Personal data is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected and to meet regulatory obligations. The standard retention period for client files is six years from the end of the engagement, in line with RICS guidance and the limitation period for contract claims.

AML records are retained for a minimum of five years from the end of the business relationship, as required by the Money Laundering Regulations 2017. Records required for tax purposes are retained in accordance with HMRC requirements.

After the retention period expires, data is securely deleted or anonymised.

08 · Your rights
What you can request.

Your rights.

Under the UK GDPR, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of accessto request a copy of the personal data held about you.
  • Right of rectificationto ask for inaccurate or incomplete data to be corrected.
  • Right of erasureto request deletion of your data, subject to legal or regulatory retention requirements.
  • Right to restrict processingto limit how your data is used in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portabilityto receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to objectto processing based on legitimate interests, including any direct marketing.
  • Right to withdraw consentwhere processing is based on consent, you can withdraw that consent at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, email rob@rhcharteredsurveyors.co.uk with "Data Subject Request" in the subject line. The practice will respond within one calendar month.

09 · Security
How data is protected.

Data security.

Appropriate technical and organisational measures are in place to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, alteration, or disclosure. This includes secure passwords, encryption where appropriate, restricted access to client files, and the use of reputable IT and cloud service providers.

In the unlikely event of a data breach affecting personal data, the practice will assess and respond in line with UK GDPR requirements, including notifying affected individuals and the Information Commissioner's Office where required.

10 · Complaints
If you're unhappy.

How to complain.

If you have any concerns about how your personal data has been handled, please contact me directly in the first instance at rob@rhcharteredsurveyors.co.uk. Most concerns can be resolved quickly through direct conversation.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK supervisory authority for data protection matters. The ICO can be contacted at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.
