What this work involves
Before the deal, during, and after.

Good opportunities move quickly.
The best ones rarely wait.

Acquiring commercial property is one of the biggest decisions a landlord makes, and the window to get it right is narrow.

My job starts before a deal is even on the table. Understanding what you're trying to build, identifying assets that fit your brief, and bringing opportunities to you through strong relationships with agents, vendors, and professional contacts across the market.

Some of the best deals I've secured for clients never reached open market competition at all.

When a deal is in front of you, I'll make sure you understand what you're buying, where the risks sit, and whether it stacks up. Where there's room to improve the deal before you commit (a better price, a stronger lease structure, a different acquisition route), that's often where the real value is.

Who this is for
Active investors and owner-occupiers.

Landlords and property companies actively building or repositioning a portfolio. Investors looking for their next asset, on or off market. Owner-occupiers considering a freehold purchase.

If you're committing significant capital to commercial property and want someone in your corner before you commit, get in touch.

How I approach it.

Six principles that shape every acquisition I'm involved in: investment, owner-occupier, single asset or portfolio.

01
An early view on the deal.
Before you commit time and legal fees, I'll tell you plainly whether it stacks up. Sometimes the smartest hour you spend is the one that stops you proceeding.
02
Look beyond the brochure.
Lease structure, income security, covenant strength, repair position, obsolescence risk, future disposability. A genuine read on the asset, not a box-tick.
03
Access beyond the open market.
A meaningful part of my acquisition work is off-market. Deals come to me through existing relationships before they reach competitive bidding. You see things others don't, and secure them before tension builds the price up.
04
Improve the deal before you commit.
Price, lease terms, term certain, break options. Often the deal you exchange on is materially better than the one first offered, if someone knows where to push.
05
Specialist input where needed.
Planning, building surveying, legal specialists drawn from my network when the deal warrants it. You get the right expertise on the right question, without managing it yourself.
06
Lead the negotiation and see it through.
Agree terms and oversee through to completion, making sure the commercial deal is properly reflected in the legal documentation, not lost between agents and solicitors.
An honest position

Sometimes the right answer is no.

Not every acquisition I advise on completes. And that's often the point. If the numbers don't work or the risks outweigh the return, you need to hear that before you exchange. I'd rather tell you a deal isn't for you and lose the instruction than let you buy something that underperforms for years.
