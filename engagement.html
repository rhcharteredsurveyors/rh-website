How instructions work

Engagement Terms.

How instructions begin, how fees work, and what to expect.
Plain English. Full Terms of Business issued before any work starts.

01 · Starting out
First conversation to instruction.

How instructions begin.

The first step is always a conversation. You tell me about the matter, I tell you whether I think I can help, and we work out roughly what's involved.

If it's a fit, I'll follow up in writing with a Letter of Engagement setting out the scope of the work and the fee basis, alongside the practice's standard Terms of Business. Nothing commences until both have been issued and the engagement is accepted by you.

This is a requirement of RICS Rule 4 and good professional practice. It also protects both sides: clarity over what's being done, on what terms, for what fee, before any work begins.

02 · Fees
Fixed where possible.

How fees are structured.

Most instructions are quoted as a fixed fee. I'd rather price the work upfront than meter it by the hour, and most landlord and tenant matters lend themselves to it. The fee is set out in the Letter of Engagement before any work commences.

Where the scope is harder to define, work may be charged on an hourly basis, agreed in writing in advance. For property agency work, fees are typically calculated as a percentage of the transaction value.

All fees are exclusive of VAT and reasonable disbursements (such as Land Registry searches, mileage, or other third-party costs incurred on the instruction).

03 · Billing
When fees are invoiced.

When fees are due.

Billing terms vary by instruction and are set out in the Letter of Engagement. Common arrangements include:

  • On completionThe default for most professional work. Single invoice issued on completion of the Services.
  • Staged billingFor longer-running matters. A proportion of the fee is invoiced at agreed milestones, with the balance due on completion.
  • On transactionFor agency work, the fee is typically due on completion of the sale or letting.

Invoices are payable within 30 days of issue.

04 · Scope
What's covered, what isn't.

Scope of services.

Each Letter of Engagement defines the scope of work specifically. As a general principle, the advice provided is limited to matters on which a Chartered Surveyor is qualified to advise.

Some matters fall outside that scope and are not covered unless specifically agreed in writing. These include:

  • Legal adviceInterpretation or drafting of leases, contracts, or other legal documents. A solicitor should be instructed for these matters.
  • Formal valuationsRed Book valuations for lender, HMRC, court or probate purposes. A RICS Registered Valuer is instructed where required.
  • Building surveysStructural surveys, condition reports, or dilapidations assessments unless specifically commissioned.
  • Specialist mattersAsbestos, contamination, capital allowances, or other matters requiring specialist consultants.

Where additional work is requested beyond the original scope, this will be agreed in writing with a corresponding fee variation.

05 · Termination
If circumstances change.

Ending an instruction.

Either party may terminate an instruction by giving 30 days' written notice. Fees and disbursements incurred up to the point of termination remain payable, calculated on a pro-rata basis where applicable.

Some specific termination provisions apply where work cannot be completed for reasons outside both parties' control, or where regulatory obligations require it. These are set out in full in the Terms of Business.

06 · Confidentiality
Information shared in confidence.

Confidentiality both ways.

Information shared in the course of an instruction is treated as confidential. The practice does not discuss client matters with third parties without authority, save where disclosure is required by professional duty, regulatory obligation, or law.

The full position on confidentiality, including in joint instructions and where information must be disclosed to professional bodies or authorities, is set out in the Terms of Business.

07 · Liability
Professional indemnity cover.

Liability limits.

The practice maintains Professional Indemnity Insurance in accordance with RICS requirements. The standard aggregate limit of liability for any single instruction is £1,000,000, reflecting the PI cover in place.

A higher limit may be agreed in writing before an instruction commences, subject to availability of insurance and an additional fee. The standard liability limit, exclusions, and other related provisions are set out in detail in the Terms of Business.

Advice is provided solely for the benefit of the Client and may not be relied upon by third parties without prior written consent.

08 · Governing law
Jurisdiction.

Governing law.

The engagement agreement, including the Letter of Engagement and Terms of Business, is governed by and construed in accordance with English law. Both parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the English Courts in relation to any dispute arising out of or in connection with the agreement.

09 · Full terms
Issued on engagement.

The full Terms of Business.

This page is a summary of the practice's standard engagement framework, intended to give prospective clients a clear understanding of how work is structured before getting in touch.

The full Letter of Engagement and Terms of Business (including detailed provisions on services, fees, liability, intellectual property, anti-money laundering, complaints, data protection, and other matters) are issued in writing before any instruction commences. A copy is available on request in advance of engagement if you'd like to see it.
