Case Study · Rates Challenge

Stapleford Industrial Unit.

Owner-occupier rates challenge following 2023 revaluation. Rateable value reduced from £15,000 to £13,750 through CCA process. Annual saving of approximately £4,000 plus transitional relief.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Industrial owner-occupied unit
  • 2023 RV increased to £15,000 (loss of SBRR)
  • Revised RV agreed at £13,750
  • Annual rates reduced from c. £7,500 to £3,000
  • Approximate saving of £4,000 per annum
  • Re-instructed for 2026 revaluation challenge
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The instruction.

I was instructed by an owner-occupier in relation to their business rates liability at Unit 1, R/O 92A Church Street, Stapleford.

Historically, the property had benefited from a rateable value below the threshold for Small Business Rates Relief (SBRR), resulting in a nil rates liability.

Following the 2023 revaluation, the rateable value increased to £15,000, pushing the property above the SBRR threshold and increasing the client's liability from £0 to approximately £7,500 per annum.

02 · Our role
What I did.

What the work involved.

A strategic review of the rateable value, assessing the likelihood of a successful challenge and identifying potential grounds for appeal.

My analysis identified a number of issues with the existing valuation, including inaccuracies in the measurement of the property, incorrect classification within the valuation scheme, and concerns regarding the valuation methodology applied.

Following my advice, the client instructed me to progress the matter through the Check, Challenge, Appeal (CCA) process with the Valuation Office Agency.

During the Check stage, the VOA accepted that the property had been incorrectly measured and amended the rateable value accordingly. I subsequently progressed to Challenge, where the VOA accepted that the property had been misclassified within the valuation scheme, although they maintained their position in relation to valuation methodology.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

The outcome.

A revised rateable value of £13,750 was agreed, bringing the property back within range to benefit from Small Business Rates Relief (in part), alongside transitional relief provisions.

This reduced the client's annual rates liability from approximately £7,500 to £3,000, delivering a saving of around £4,000 per annum, in addition to further savings through transitional relief.

Following the 2026 revaluation, the rateable value has increased again to £17,250. I have been re-instructed to challenge the new assessment, with the outcome currently pending.
