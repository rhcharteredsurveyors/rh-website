What it is
The handbook.

Most of the cost and risk in a commercial lease is settled before anyone puts pen to paper. The earlier you understand it, the more room you have to do something about it.

Your premises are probably your second biggest cost after people, and a lease commits you to that cost for years, usually before you have had a real chance to understand what you are signing. This handbook sets out the parts of a lease that cost businesses money and cause them grief: what to look for, what to ask, and where the real risks sit.

It does not replace advice, and it is not meant to. It is the thing you read first, so that when you do pick up the phone, you are asking better questions and paying for advice on the things that matter rather than the basics. It covers commercial property in England and Wales. The law is different in Scotland and Northern Ireland.

  1. 01

    Before you sign

    Heads of terms, the questions to ask before you agree anything, and the red flags that mean stop and take advice.

  2. 02

    The cost of occupation

    Rent, service charge, insurance and rates, and the gap between a headline figure and what occupying the building really costs.

  3. 03

    Lease events

    Rent reviews, renewals and security of tenure, break clauses, assignment and subletting, and what you are allowed to do with the space.

  4. 04

    The building and its condition

    Repairs and dilapidations, the schedule of condition, fit-out and reinstatement, and the energy rules that decide whether a building can be let at all.

  5. 05

    Taking advice

    Deposits, guarantees and what you sign up to personally, and who does what when a lease is on the table.

It comes with request templates, a plain-English glossary, and a separate checklist pack: twelve working checklists to print and fill in, from a heads of terms review sheet to a red flags list to run through before you sign.
