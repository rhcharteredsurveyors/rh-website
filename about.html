The story so far
From graduate to founder.

I'm Rob Haigh, a chartered surveyor, a proud husband and father of three, and the person you'll work with directly if you instruct RH Chartered Surveyors.

I graduated from Nottingham Trent University with a degree in Real Estate Management BSc (Hons) in 2012, and started my career at Fisher German, where I qualified as a chartered surveyor in 2014. I acted as a general practitioner which gave me a grounding across every discipline in commercial property.

I then moved to Harris Lamb, and from there to Lambert Smith Hampton, where I was appointed Associate Director — the youngest in the company at the time. Both firms are more departmentally structured, and I worked specifically in property agency, almost exclusively for landlord clients. I learned the discipline of corporate work, the volume, the pressure, the standards. And I learned, from the inside, what gets lost when a client's relationship is divided up between specialist teams.

In 2019, I founded RH Chartered Surveyors. I wanted to build a practice where clients got a proper relationship: one person who knew the whole picture, stayed with them across every commercial property matter that came up, and treated the work as a long-term partnership rather than a series of transactions.

Today, RH Chartered Surveyors is a founder-led practice working with both landlords and tenants across the country, on the full range of general practice surveying. Some clients come to me for one piece of work; others have me as their lead advisor across an entire portfolio. The structure scales to what each client needs, and the relationship stays the same throughout.

How I work
One advisor. One relationship.

When you hire me, you get me. From the first conversation to the final completion, and usually for a long time after that.

I'm a general practice surveyor, which means I advise across every discipline in commercial property. So if you come to me for a lease renewal and then need a view on an acquisition, a rates appeal, or a strategic review of your portfolio, you carry on with me. One relationship, broadening as you need it to.

When something falls outside what I do — dilapidations reporting, building surveys, legal work, Red Book valuations — I'll tell you plainly and introduce you to someone in my network I'd trust with my own affairs.

You get the specialist; you also keep me as your point of contact for everything else.

What I stand for.

Five principles that have shaped this practice since the day I founded it — and that shape every instruction I take on.

01
Straight talking.
I'll tell you what I think, including when it's not what you want to hear. Commercial property decisions often come down to judgement calls, and judgement is only useful when it's honestly given.
02
Real availability.
When you need me, you ring me. My mobile is the same number I give every client. WhatsApp if that's easier, email if it works better, in person if preferred — and if I miss a call, I'll come back to you as soon as I can.
03
Long-term thinking.
A lease renewal done badly can quietly cost you for years. A rushed acquisition can underperform for a generation. I'd rather take the time to advise properly than rush a decision just to close a file.
04
No machinery.
I don't have departments. I don't have margins to hit. I don't have a partner to report numbers to. What I have is a practice I built on purpose — so the advice you get is shaped by what's right for you, not what's right for an annual target.
05
Professional standards.
RH Chartered Surveyors is regulated by the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS) and operates in accordance with the RICS Rules of Conduct. Professional indemnity insurance and formal complaints procedures are in place.
