Case Study · Rates Reduction

Business Rates Appeal, York.

Owner-occupier challenge to RV classification successfully reduced rateable value from £16,750 to £11,250, bringing the property below the SBRR threshold. Rates liability cut from £8,000+ per annum to £nil.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Owner-occupied commercial property in York
  • RV reduced from £16,750 to £11,250 (33% reduction)
  • Property brought below SBRR threshold
  • Rates liability reduced from £8,000+ pa to £nil
  • Advice on billing position and backdating exposure
  • Successful CCA challenge through VOA
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The instruction.

I was instructed by a private client to review the business rates position for their owner-occupied property in York.

Despite being in occupation for a number of years, the client had not yet received a business rates bill from the local authority. This created a risk of a significant backdated liability once the property was formally assessed.

The client required advice both on managing this exposure and on the accuracy of the rateable value applied to the property.

02 · Our role
What I did.

What the work involved.

I initially advised the client on how to engage with the local authority to regularise the billing position, ensuring that the start date for the rating assessment was agreed on terms that minimised the client's exposure to backdated liability.

I then undertook a detailed review of the rateable value and identified a number of issues with the assessment, including incorrect classification of the property as retail, rather than offices, and concerns regarding the valuation methodology applied.

Although the property featured a prominent glazed frontage, I demonstrated that its location outside York's primary retail area, combined with limited footfall and reliance on passing traffic, made it more appropriately classified as office accommodation.

Following my advice, the client instructed me to proceed with a challenge through the Check, Challenge, Appeal (CCA) process with the Valuation Office Agency.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

The outcome.

Following detailed negotiations with the VOA, my key arguments were accepted and the rateable value was reduced from £16,750 to £11,250, representing a reduction of approximately 33%.

Crucially, this brought the property below the threshold for Small Business Rates Relief, reducing the client's potential liability from over £8,000 per annum to £nil.

I continue to advise the client on their business rates position, including strategies to maintain eligibility for relief and minimise future liabilities.
