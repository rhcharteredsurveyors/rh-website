What most tenants don't know
The rules of the game.

Most tenants are unrepresented at rent review or lease renewal, and most don't realise there are rules the landlord has to follow.

They assume surveyors only act for landlords. Or that professional representation isn't something a smaller business can justify. Or that there's nothing to negotiate because the landlord sets the number.

None of that is right.

Rent reviews and lease renewals are governed by RICS rules that exist specifically to protect both sides.

A landlord can't simply pick a rent. They have to justify it with proper market evidence, conduct the negotiation fairly, and abide by clear rules on what they have to show you and what evidence they're entitled to rely on.

Without representation, most tenants don't know any of this, so they fall back on the only argument they think they have: that their business can't afford an increase. Emotional appeals rarely move the needle, because they don't engage the rules the negotiation is actually being conducted under. What does move the needle is someone on your side who understands those rules as well as the landlord's surveyor does.

How I approach it.

Six principles that shape every tenant lease renewal and rent review I take on, for any premises, in any market, of any complexity.

01
Read every document first.
Lease, variations, side letters, correspondence. I know what you've signed up to before I speak to your landlord.
02
Assess the proposal honestly.
Whether the number is reasonable, high, or unsupportable, and whether the evidence stacks up. Sometimes there's a fight worth having. Sometimes there isn't. I'll tell you which.
03
Build the case.
Market evidence, comparable transactions, condition of the premises, lease terms, sector context. The argument is only as strong as the evidence underneath it.
04
Lead the negotiation directly.
Direct conversations with your landlord's agent. You kept informed at every step, at the level of detail that suits you.
05
See it through.
Heads of terms, solicitor liaison, signed paperwork. The job isn't done until the paperwork is signed.
06
Act early.
Lease events reward preparation. Coming to me well before the next event lets us position your situation properly, not react to someone else's framing of it.
What good outcomes look like

Lower rent. Smarter terms.
Real certainty.

Every instruction is different. But most tenant work comes down to some combination of:

  • 01

    A rent lower than first proposed.

    Whether through evidence-based negotiation, stepped uplifts, or rent-free periods structured to absorb the impact.

  • 02

    A lease structured around your flexibility.

    Break clauses, repair obligations, and renewal rights that work for the business, not just the landlord.

  • 03

    Terms that protect you.

    Personal guarantees removed where appropriate. Payment terms set to match cash flow. Service charge caps where they make sense.

  • 04

    Certainty over your cost base.

    So you can plan, invest, and grow the business with confidence rather than anxiety about the next rent demand.
