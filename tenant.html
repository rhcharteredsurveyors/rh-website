The asymmetry
Why independent advice matters.

Commercial property is a system that works fairly when both sides are properly represented.

The reality is that most tenants aren't. They assume surveyors only act for landlords, or that proper representation isn't something their business can justify, and they end up negotiating alone against landlords and agents who do this every week.

That gap in experience is real. The other side does this professionally, full-time, with specialists for every angle. You don't. Which means most occupiers walk into these moments at a structural disadvantage — and pay for it in rent, in terms, in flexibility, and in long-term cost.

I spent the first part of my career at Fisher German, Harris Lamb, and Lambert Smith Hampton — working almost exclusively for landlords. I know what landlords will push for, where they'll compromise, and which arguments actually shift a position.

When you instruct me, that knowledge works for you.

I act exclusively for the occupier in tenant instructions. Same expertise as the landlord's advisors — used to level the playing field, not lean on it.

Some tenants come to me for a single lease event. Others have me as their lead advisor across multiple premises over time. The structure scales to what you need — and either way, the relationship is the same.
