Case Study · Lease Restructure & Re-letting

145 Nottingham Road, Mansfield.

Restructured a failed lease assignment into a new direct lease. Comprehensive tenant improvement package secured, asset protected, ongoing management retained.

At a glance
Key facts & figures.
  • Retail property in Mansfield
  • Lease assignment failed twice
  • New lease negotiated at market rent
  • Comprehensive tenant improvement works secured
  • Three-month rent free period agreed
  • Ongoing property management instruction retained
01 · Overview
The instruction context.

The instruction.

I was instructed by the landlord of 145 Nottingham Road, Mansfield to oversee the proposed assignment of the existing lease.

The occupational tenant intended to return to their home country due to ill-health and had agreed terms to assign the lease to a new occupier. The transaction progressed to the point of completion before the proposed assignee withdrew at the final stage.

I subsequently secured an alternative assignee and agreed terms for the lease assignment. However, before the assignment could complete, the existing tenant vacated the premises and left the country.

02 · Our role
What I did.

What the work involved.

Active oversight of the proposed assignment process, including qualifying the incoming tenant, negotiating terms between the parties, and coordinating the transaction with the respective legal advisers.

When the existing tenant vacated the property and left the country, the assignment process could no longer proceed. At this stage I pivoted the strategy and entered into negotiations with the proposed assignee for a new lease directly with the landlord.

The incoming tenant had already been vetted as a suitable occupier, however an inspection of the property following the tenant's departure revealed the premises to be in a significantly dilapidated condition.

Given the tenant had left the country, I advised the landlord that the likelihood of successfully recovering dilapidations from the previous occupier was extremely limited. The priority therefore became securing a viable occupational arrangement that worked for both the landlord, who is based overseas, and the incoming tenant who could not operate from the property in its existing condition.

I negotiated a practical package of works, comprising minor landlord works together with a comprehensive schedule of tenant improvements to bring the property back into a compliant and operational standard. A three-month rent free period was agreed to offset part of the tenant's investment in carrying out these works.

03 · Outcome
What the client got.

The outcome.

A new occupational lease was successfully completed at market rent, securing a tenant for the property despite the collapse of the original assignment process.

The agreed programme of works ensured the building was improved and brought back into a suitable condition for occupation, protecting the landlord's asset and enabling the tenant to commence trading.

I continue to act for the landlord, an international client, providing ongoing property management and advisory services in relation to the property.
