What this work involves
A view on value tied to a real decision.

What's this asset actually worth?

What would it fetch on the open market today? What's it worth in two years if I do X? What would a special purchaser pay for it? How does the lease structure affect value? Where's the upside?

This is the work landlords need most often.

A commercial view, properly evidenced and written for you rather than for a lender's file, supports real decisions in a way that a formal valuation report doesn't.

Who this is for
Landlords facing decisions where value matters.

Landlords facing a decision where value is the critical unknown. An asset you're considering selling. A portfolio you're refinancing. An acquisition where the price needs testing. A dispute where an independent view of value matters.

If you need to understand what your property is worth before you commit to a course of action, get in touch.

How I approach it.

Six principles that shape every valuation instruction, whether a single asset or a portfolio-wide review.

01
Start with what you actually need.
Valuation advice tied to a specific decision is where the real value sits. The first conversation is usually about the decision, not the number.
02
Multiple scenarios.
Value today. Value assuming re-letting. Value with asset management improvements. Value to a special purchaser. Different numbers for different questions.
03
Backed by evidence.
Comparable transactions, market data, sector tone, lease analysis. Every number traces back to evidence, not assumption.
04
Work through the variables.
Lease terms, covenant strength, repair position, market tone, alternative-use potential, special purchaser considerations. What's actually driving value.
05
Explain it plainly.
You should finish the conversation understanding not just the number, but why it's the number. The reasoning matters as much as the figure.
06
The right specialist when needed.
Some situations call for a formal Red Book valuation prepared by a RICS Registered Valuer. When that's what you need, I'll introduce you to one I'd trust with my own affairs.
An honest position

Written for you. Not a lender's file.

A formal Red Book valuation has its place: lender requirements, HMRC submissions, probate, court proceedings. It's a compliance product, written to a regulated standard for a third party.

Strategic valuation advice is different. It's written for you, focused on the decision you're facing, and built to be useful, not just defensible. That's the work landlords need most often.
