What this work involves
Real challenges, real evidence.

Business rates are one of the biggest ongoing costs of occupying commercial property.

And they're frequently wrong. Rateable values are set by the Valuation Office Agency using methods that can produce errors. The appeal process (Check, Challenge, Appeal) is designed for occupiers to push back where the assessment doesn't stack up.

My role is to tell you honestly whether your assessment is worth challenging, and to run the process properly when it is.

Equally importantly, I'll tell you when it isn't. A failed appeal costs you time, fees, and sometimes a higher assessment. Knowing when not to challenge is often more valuable than knowing when to.

Who this is for
Tenants and businesses paying rates.

Tenants and business owners who think their rateable value might be wrong. Businesses that have had a revaluation and aren't sure whether to challenge it. Occupiers who want an independent view before committing to a process they don't fully understand.

If you're paying rates and you're not certain you're paying the right amount, get in touch.

How I approach it.

Six principles that shape every tenant rates instruction, from initial review through to final outcome.

01
Review before you commit.
A proper assessment of whether the rateable value is likely to be excessive, based on measurement, classification, methodology, and comparable evidence.
02
An honest cost-benefit view.
Likely saving versus likely time, fees, and risk. No appeal pursued for appeal's sake, and no fee charged for work that won't deliver value.
03
Run the CCA process properly.
Check, Challenge, and Appeal stages, with evidence built correctly at each step and submitted in the format the VOA actually requires.
04
Mitigation where it matters.
Relocation, downsizing, reconfiguration, transitional relief, occupation strategy across multiple premises, eligibility for Small Business Rates Relief.
05
Flag when not to appeal.
Often the most valuable advice on a rates review is "don't." Some assessments are broadly right. Some carry technical routes that would trigger reassessment and worse exposure.
06
Joined-up advice across premises.
If you occupy more than one property, rates strategy needs to consider the whole picture. What's right for one site may not be right for another, and decisions on one can affect your position elsewhere.
An honest position

Knowing when not to challenge.

Several of my most valued rates instructions have ended in me advising the client to do nothing. That advice is worth paying for, because it protects you from costly mistakes that less careful advisors might happily talk you into.

Some assessments are broadly right. Some carry a technical route to savings that could trigger a reassessment and increase your liability instead. In either case, you'll have paid a modest fee for proper advice and saved yourself the far larger cost of pursuing the wrong path.
