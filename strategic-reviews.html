Strategic Reviews are often the easiest way to start working together. You get a considered view on a specific situation. I get to understand how you think.

If further instructions flow from it, they flow.

What this work involves
The whole position, named honestly.

Sometimes the right next step isn't obvious.

The lease renewal is coming up and the tenant's been difficult. The asset's underperforming and you're not sure whether to manage through it, let it go, or restructure. You've got multiple leases on one building that don't quite make sense together. You're eyeing a freehold purchase but can't see the full picture.

In those moments, what you need isn't a transaction.

You need a focused piece of independent advice that looks at the whole position, names the risks and opportunities honestly, and helps you decide what to do. That's what a Strategic Review is.

Who this is for
Landlords with a decision to make.

Landlords facing a decision that isn't clear cut. An asset that's underperforming. A lease event with a difficult tenant. A disposal you're not sure about. A situation where you'd value an independent view before committing to a course of action.

If you're wrestling with something and want a plain read on where you stand, get in touch.

How I approach it.

Six principles that shape every Strategic Review I undertake, regardless of asset type or sector.

01
Understand what you're actually deciding.
The framing of the question determines the usefulness of the answer. The first conversation is usually about getting that framing right.
02
Look at the full picture.
Lease structures, market context, tenant position, asset performance, upcoming risks, realistic options. Not just the immediate question, but everything around it.
03
Name what matters.
What's actually driving the decision, where the risks sit, where the opportunities are. The signal, separated from the noise.
04
Show the working.
You don't just get the conclusion. You get the reasoning, the evidence, and the assumptions underneath it. So you can challenge it, build on it, or revisit it later.
05
Give you a plain recommendation.
A strategic review that ends in "it depends" isn't a review. I'll tell you what I think, why, and what I'd do if it were mine.
06
Leave the door open.
If the review points to further work (renewal negotiations, a disposal, restructuring), I'm there to take it on. If it points to "do nothing", that's a valid and often valuable answer too.
An honest position

Paid advice, properly delivered.

A Strategic Review is a charged piece of work. That matters, because it means the advice is genuinely independent. Not shaped by the prospect of a transaction fee at the end. You're paying for thinking, and you get exactly that.
